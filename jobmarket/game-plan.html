Caution

All opinions in this workshop are mine and personal. They may be different from the opinions of your advisors and other professors. You have to form your own opinions in this process. I receive no funding from the department and benefit little from doing this workshop other than providing a service that I think should be provided. Just trying to be a bro here, please don't get me into any trouble for doing this workshop.

I also expect you to give back to this workshop. This means that you will support other participants in finding a job and share your thoughts and materials for future participants. Read other people's drafts and attend other people's practice job talks. Don't be an asshole.

Rules

We will meet weekly or biweekly on Wednesday via Zoom. These discussion sessions will take place on from 1 to 3 PM with a 15-minute break. I will distribute a Zoom invitation before the meeting. These meetings are for discussion about a certain topic, not lectures. Please prepare thoughtful questions for the group to discuss.

Unless there are special circumstances, you are expected to find a quiet indoor space to attend the meetings. A participant is responsible to take note for the meeting and send the note to me within a week. Be ready to screen share.

Before each meeting, you are expected to complete the readings and assignment. Please upload your drafts only in the format of Google Doc or PDF. Please comment directly online so all participants can view them.

Be friendly, professional, and civil. Respect other people's time and effort. Don't be defensive, as you don't have the opportunity to debate when you're looking for a job. The committee decides whether you should be interviewed and there's no appeal.

Teams

I think it's best to break the workshop into three teams so people are not overwhelmed by their feedback duty. You are, however, free to give additional comments if you're interested in reading any specific file.

Below you will see the team assignments. My goal is to diversify these teams so there is a variety of intellectual interests in each team. Remember, your materials will be read by committee members who are not at all familiar with your areas or methods. You are responsible to read and comment on all the materials submitted by your team members. We will adjust these teams when needed.

* These students will not submit their own materials but comment on other team members' materials.

** On maternal leave and will only attend part of the workshop.

Infrastructure

We will use Google Drive to share documents. You are not allowed to share any material from this workshop without the explicit agreement of the author. There are three primary folders:

  • Upload: where you upload your own drafts and other assignments.
  • Reading: where I store supplementary readings. If you find anything useful, please email it to me so I can upload it to this folder.
  • Examples: where you can find examples shared by faculty and alumni.

You should already have the access to these folders.
