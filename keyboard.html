These are Some of My Favorite Things

It has been about a year since I entered the keyboard community!

Looking back, my first post in r/MechanicalKeyboards was on September 20th, 2019. At that point, I just bought an Anne Pro 2 but couldn't wait to try something new. Little did I know that I would end of trying so many different keyboards and now have a collection.

Here I document my collection and the crazy journey!

FC660C: The First Endgame

SPECS: FC660C + 45g stock domes + Heavy 6 Motorsport + Purple Sliders + Hasu Controller + EC9009

Like many newbies, I was curious about why Topre has so many diehard fans. After much hesitation, I decided to pull the trigger and get an FC660C. I was aware of HHKB at the time but thought I could not live without arrow keys.

The Oneness legend is true! I enjoyed the type feel instantly. Compared to many other switches, Topre just felt much more elegant and understated. Topre was also particularly attractive to me at that time because I had used membrane keyboard all my life. A soft bottom out was what I was used to.

I liked the board so much I started hunting for an affordable Heavy-6 on r/mechmarket. It was probably the most frivolous item I had ever tried to buy. I ended up getting a bundle of Heavy-6, FC660C, and these caps from u/JapaneseHorrorwriter at a very good price. Thank you for making my first "end-game" dream come true!

I have tried all sorts of mods with this build, including 55g and BKE dome swap. Among which, purple sliders made the most difference. The silenced Topre is an even more sophisticated Topre! Hasu controller also adds a lot of flexibility now I am more used to the HHKB layout. Even though I don't use this board as much these days because of its layout, I will always keep this board.

Modern Model F62: One Man, One Dream, One Masterpiece

SPECS: Modern Model F62 HHKB Layout + Unicomp Red Accent Caps + Aviator Connector + IBM sticker

Getting tired of all the nice photos on r/MechanicalKeyboards, I began to go on GeekHack and Deskthority, where I learned a lot about vintage keyboards and learned about Joe's recreation of Model F.

The more I read about it, the more I realized how crazy/ambitious this project was. This is truly the most epic GB I have ever heard! I believe people had been waiting for 3 to 4 years for the GB to deliver when I discovered it. Everyone was anxious about whether Model F could really be recreated.

I was lucky enough to join the buy right when the manufacturing was done and received my Modern Model F77 after only two months of wait. This board is simply a piece of art and a testimony to the ingenuity and passion of this community. Joe not only recreated Model F, he creates a board that is even better than the original models.

Modern Model F is really the best thing money can buy in this hobby (<$400 for everything including the zinc case). I recommend everyone who's interested in Model M or F just skip those boards and get this one.

Buckling spring switches, to me, are the exact opposite of Topre. One is fire, the other is water. One is sun, the other is moon. Bucking spring is loud and aggressive. It makes a statement. The zinc case Joe designed only enhance all these fiery qualities.

As I realized I prefer 60%, I got the board you see and sold my F77. Again, this is a board I will keep.

IBM 5251 Beamspring Keyboard: The Majestic

SPECS: IBM 5251 + xwhatsit controller + IBM Selectric Caps

My obsession with vintage boards did not stop at buckling spring. Besides lurking on Deskthority and Geekhack, I was also watching tons of Chyrosran's Youtube videos. He really destroyed my paycheck at the time.

My attraction to vintage boards was not due to the look but the variety of switches out there. They just type so differently from all the MX variants, especially those with a more complicated structure.

During this period, I frequented eBay, Goodwill's website, and local craigslist, hoping to find something for a bargain price. I was lucky to find an IBM Model F XT, Model M SSK, P70, Pingmaster, Apple M0110, and M0116.

Converting vintage boards was challenging and a lot of fun. I had to learn how keyboards actually work, electrically speaking. I also gained a deep appreciation of the history because many things are now possible because all the experiments that have been done. The problem-solving spirit in the community is really what makes it special.

The natural ending of this journey is what you see—the glorious IBM beamspring! I noticed that someone in Portugal was trying to offload this board for a few months. He posted it on Deskthority and reddit quite a few times but no one took the bite, perhaps because shipping this thing across the Atlantic is very risky and, as you can see, there's a cosmetic defect on the spacebar.

I decided to go for it because the price was the lowest I have seen. After learning how much BS boards were sold on eBay in the past few months, I also knew I couldn't find a more affordable options.

It arrived safely and fully functional, thanks to the amazing packaging. It's almost like dissecting a mummy when I opened the box. Beamspring did not disappoint with the unique collapse in its force curve. This is the grail to me!

I am hoping the development of Keystone Beamspring switches will be successful so more people can have the experience!

Blue, Orange, and Brown: Alps builds!

SPECS: Royal Glam Wood Case + Blue Alps + Nexus Sliders + ePBT 9009

The only vintage switches that are customizable these days are Alps. I can totally see why! Tactile and clicky Alps are simply something that no MX switches can replicate. I still haven't tried Amber because they are very expensive.

My favorite thing about Alps switches is their roundness. MX switches are basically a piece of plastic moving along a straight line, sometimes with disruption. The tactility of Alps switches is created by bent metal, which has more flex. I guess the same about buckling spring switches.

Because of this, I do not find linear Alps to be desirable over MX linear switches. The MX switches were originally designed to be linear and all the new clones, in my opinion, are as smooth as linear Alps.

SPECS: Modern M0110 + Orange Alps + Nexus Sliders + Rama Heavy Industry

To be perfectly honest, Alps was the reason I learned to solder. Before Alps, I could get by with hot-swap boards but there are no sockets for Alps switches. To repeat, what millions of people have said: it's really not that hard!

I again got really lucky with this Modern M0110. This was another GB that took a long time to fulfill and I was able to get one at the original price on Geekhack. Many designers have been inspired by M0110 but I think Modern M0110 is the best, most true-to-its-origin iteration.

SPECS: Keyboardbelle ADK Case + Brown Alps + Nexus Sliders + ePBT Extended 2048

The third build is so unoriginal, it's almost a rite of passage for people who are into Alps. I am not actually an Apple user but the idea of building a 60% AEK board is irresistible. It took a while for me to get all the parts but it is now complete!

As you may have noticed, I use BlindAssassin's Nexus Sliders and ePBT caps for all three boards. This is because I was married to the HHKB layout at this point, and it is simply impossible find Alps caps for this layout. They also work great together!

Currently semi-retired from ALPS. If someone runs another GB for modern ALPS caps, I will be back...

Klippe S: Firm & Calm

SPECS: Klippe S HHKB + Holy Blue Ink + GMK Space Cadet R1 Blue Base

I did get some MX boards before I entered the world of vintage boards. One of the best I got was a Klippe. It was a simple tray-mount but the quality did speak for itself.

This was why I got really curious about Klippe S and was searching for a couple months to purchase one. There were not many Klippe S in the world compared to other tray-mount Mekanisk boards.

The bottom-mount design provides a firm and even type feel. There is a unique unity about this keyboard. I am certainly following LeandreN's products and may pick up a Tind down the road.

HHKB Pro Hybrid Type S: Stock Perfection

SPECS: HHKB Pro Hybrid Type S + Custom Redwood Spacebar & Control + FC660C Enter

Realizing that I actually love HHKB layout, I decided to get an HHKB when the third generation was released.

It has been the keyboard I use when I travel, which has not been happening too much this year, because of its weight. It is also the board I use when I am trouble-shooting a build because its Bluetooth functionality. Because of these reasons, I probably won't get the holy grail for this board. It needs to stay portable and versatile.

Moontower & 512: The Austin 60s!

 

SPECS: Moontower Pirate Black + Vint Black with Outemu Top Housing + GMK Olivia Dark

SPECS: 512 + Gateron Giant + GMK Café (built by OCM)

These are no doubt the most sought-after boards in my collection, if we judge their popularity by market price. What really special to me is that they are designed by an Austin designer chanchan. People in Austin love our city!

I was too late to join the raffle for 512 GB and lost the raffle for Moontower GB. However, I got extremely lucky. I joined the B-stock raffle for Moontower and won the only unit! I was also able to buy 512 from OCM, who was only selling to people in Austin for $200 and some local beer.

Texas Discord Server is the best. Period. People are all super welcoming on the server and it is truly a haven from all the cruelty in this hobby. I guess being part of this server was the luckiest thing that ever happened to me in this hobby.

I don't really see the day that I sell two keyboards unless I lose all my fingers or something. If that day comes, I will certainly keep these two boards in Austin, because only Austinites will fully appreciate these boards.

Yes, I saved the raffle video. I still can't believe it.

 

Under the Sea: A Glass Keyboard

SPECS: Fused Glass Case + O-Ring Mount + Moyu/Dark Jade + GMK Coral

For the longest time I wondered what a keyboard made out of glass would look like. Knowing that it is unlikely anyone would run a GB with a glass case, I decided to contact a local glass workshops and see if they were willing to do a commission work.

This is the final product! It has some obvious imperfection because it's entirely hand-made, and, because of the pandemic, I couldn't be more involved in the process than I would like. However, the case is fully functional with o-ring mount. Pairing with Moyu switches, the sound is clear and crisp.

GMK Coral is the first GMK GB I joined. Even though it wasn't particularly popular, there's something special about this set that pushed me into the GMK rabbit hole. This is why I put the set on this special keyboard. It's also great that this set benefited the Coral Reef Alliance in Australia.
